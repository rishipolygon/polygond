Reading the Curve

What the shape of a forward curve actually tells you about the world.

By Polygond · May 21, 2026

A forward curve is a story the market tells itself about the future. When near-dated contracts trade below later ones — contango — the market is saying it would rather have the commodity later than now. When the curve inverts into backwardation, the opposite holds: scarcity today commands a premium.

The shape matters more than the level. A flat curve drifting into backwardation is often a louder signal than a headline price print.

The curve will tell you who is anxious and who is comfortable.

Why it pays to watch

Storage economics, carry costs, and the real cost of holding inventory are all baked into that slope. Read it carefully and the curve becomes a sentiment gauge that updates in real time — long before the commentary catches up.
