Cheap Money, Expensive Lessons

Every cycle teaches the same lesson in a new accent.

By Polygond · May 14, 2026

When capital is cheap, discipline feels optional. Marginal projects get funded, marginal companies get bid up, and the cost of being wrong is quietly socialised across a rising market.

Then the rate moves. Suddenly the same project that pencilled at four percent does not pencil at eight, and the difference between a good business and a leveraged one becomes very clear, very fast.

Leverage is a wonderful servant and a terrible master.

The accent changes, not the lesson

The lesson never changes. Only the accent does — and the people who have to learn it. Each generation arrives convinced that this time the arithmetic is different, and each one discovers, eventually, that it never was.
