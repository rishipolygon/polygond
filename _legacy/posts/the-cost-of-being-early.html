The Cost of Being Early

Being right and being early are not the same trade.

By Polygond · May 28, 2026

Being right and being early are not the same trade. The market does not pay out on foresight; it pays out on timing, and the gap between the two is where most good ideas quietly die.

A thesis can be correct in every particular and still ruin you, because the position has to survive long enough for the world to agree. Conviction without liquidity is just a slow-motion margin call.

The graveyard of investing is full of people who were right too soon.

So the discipline is not in finding the idea. It is in sizing it so you are still standing when it finally works — and in being honest about how long that might take.

Patience as a position

The hardest position to hold is the one that does nothing for months. It earns no praise, shows no progress, and tempts you, every single day, to do something. Most of the time, the right move is to keep holding — and the cost of being early is simply the toll you pay for that patience.
